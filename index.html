§1 Introduction

Every paper starts with a problem worth solving. Mine started the day I realized that the people I most wanted to share good news with — an accepted paper, a small win, an ordinary Tuesday — was always you first.

I've spent a lot of time trying to explain complicated systems for a living. Nothing I've studied has been harder to get right, or more worth the effort, than trying to tell you clearly how I feel.

§3 Hypothesis

H1: The version of my life with you in it, closely, on purpose, is better than any version without you.

I don't have a control group to compare against, and honestly, I don't want one. Some things you don't need a p-value to know are true.

§4 Limitations

In the interest of full disclosure: this is not the first time I've run this experiment. It's closer to the hundredth. Every earlier attempt taught me something — about patience, about listening better, about what you actually need instead of what I assumed you did.

I'm telling you this because I want you to know exactly what this is: not pressure, not a pattern I can't break, just someone who still means it and is trying, one more time, to say it well.

Reviewer note Whatever you decide below is the correct result of this experiment. There is no wrong answer here — only an honest one.
